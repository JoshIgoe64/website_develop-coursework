1.1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business
The main purpose of IT in business is improving relationships with customers, this allows us to make their lives easier for an online vendor and delivery, use social media to increase exposure and foster an advertising platform with lower costs and complementary to its physical counterpart (Billboards, TV Adverts, etc.). This also makes it easier for customers to spend money online as they feel more disjointed from it, thus have less emotional attachment and are more likely to spend. IT is also used to gauge the reception of your brand (Do people feel strongly seeing it? Is it controversial?) using sentiment analysis and big data.

1.1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?
Many digital projects will fail due to being too ambitious, companies may ask for a project that cannot be completed, e.g a full functioning hosting website with cloud integration, servers up and pay integration within 3 months from a small number of devs. They may also not understand skillsets of their workers, presuming all programmers do the same job and hire with this mentality and may end up with no agile frameworks or proper structure, meaning little productivity is made. Fixed plans can also be an issue, going against tested philosophies such as the spotify framework of incremental updates rolled out to a small number of users for testing of new features without damaging the full infrastructure.

1.1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?
    Legal guidelines can be extremely difficult to deal with in a digital project, some issues in this include:
  • Copyright- Many uses of images online may not fall under fair use as they are being used to sell a monetised product; not in education, as criticism, parody or any other exception. Many, many things fall under intellectual property, which cannot be used without the users permission.
  • The right to be forgotten- In the UK, one right of everyone is a request of the right to be forgotten, companies have to follow this per user who asks so need to implement a way of wiping all of one specific users data.
  • GDPR is also an issue as it is now enforceable (4% annual revenue), and not a guideline meaning companies have to disclose what data they are processing, show that a specific users info cannot be identified to them and they can change it and opt out of processing. However, the UK maynot need to comply to this post-Brexit.
