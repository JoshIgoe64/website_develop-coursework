1.1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business
The main purpose of IT in business is improving relationships with customers, this allows us to make their lives easier for an online vendor and delivery, use social media to increase exposure and foster an advertising platform with lower costs and complementary to its physical counterpart (Billboards, TV Adverts, etc.). This also makes it easier for customers to spend money online as they feel more disjointed from it, thus have less emotional attachment and are more likely to spend. 1.1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?
Many digital projects will fail due to being too ambitious, companies may ask for a project that cannot be completed, e.g a full functioning hosting website with cloud integration, servers up and pay integration within 3 months from a small number of devs. They may also not understand skillsets of their workers, presuming all programmers do the same job and hire with this mentality and may end up with no agile frameworks or proper structure, meaning little productivity is made. Fixed plans can also be an issue, going against tested philosophies such as the spotify framework of incremental updates rolled out to a small number of users for testing of new features without damaging the full infrastructure. Unhappy programmers can also result in impaired productivity, whether this is due to deadlines, pressure, or possible external factors.

1.1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?
    Legal guidelines can be extremely difficult to deal with in a digital project, some issues in this include:
  • Copyright- Many uses of images online may not fall under fair use as they are being used to sell a monetised product; not in education, as criticism, parody or any other exception. Many, many things fall under intellectual property, which cannot be used without the users permission.
  • The right to be forgotten- In the UK, one right of everyone is a request of the right to be forgotten, companies have to follow this per user who asks so need to implement a way of wiping all of one specific users data.
  • GDPR is also an issue as it is now enforceable (4% annual revenue), and not a guideline meaning companies have to disclose what data they are processing, show that a specific users info cannot be identified to them and they can change it and opt out of processing. However, the UK maynot need to comply to this post-Brexit.

I could have been more efficient by working in a team, playing to others strengths, as I am weaker at CSS and a mentor directly on this project would aid my learning of the concepts in this language faster. A scrum system could have also been used in the team, going for small gains to keep motivation, and also allow the team to adapt faster to clients needs and achieve the optimum that can be achieved in a specific amount of time. The scrum framework is a agile system of team collaboration based on team reflection and small successes. Stand ups in this system in a team are structured, with key questions being: What did we do yesterday?, How can we improve it?; What are we doing today?, How can we optimise our productivity?; What do we need to do tomorrow?.
1.3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency
I could have improved productivity and efficiency by using Parkinsons Law and timing my tasks, or completing an Eisenhower Grid in the morning; a way of organising with a square split up into four to prioritise tasks as shown below. This method is a 2*2 grid with each of the two boxes on the left being urgent taks and each on the top being important tasks. You can then delegate non important urgent tasks, do urgent important tasks, give important but non urgent tasks low priority and get rid of non urgent, non important tasks.
